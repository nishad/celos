Module I - Network theorem: Thevenin, Norton and maximum power transfer theorems, transients in linear circuits, characteristics of signals, unit step function, impulse and ramp function, frequency spectrum of periodic waveform, discrete and continuous frequency spectra

Module II - Laplace transform analyses of simple network, partial fraction expansion, initial and final value theorems, convolution integrals, network functions, complex frequency, impedance and admittance, transfer function relation between transfer function and impulse response and their use in network analysis, zero plot

Module III - Relation between bandwidth and rise time, delay time and network function, network synthesis, synthesis of passive one port network, LC, RC, RL network, Foster and Causer methods

Module IV - Modulation techniques, amplitude modulation and demodulation, frequency dicision multiplexing, frequency modulation and demodulation, FM Radio, stereophonic FM broadcasting, B/W and colour transmission and reception

Module V - PCM, sampling, quantizing, coding, DPCM, delta modulation, digital multiplexing for telephony, digital modulation, ASK, FSK, PSK, QPSK, signal to noise ratio, AM and FM receiver models, noise in AM and FM reception


References:

2. Network Analysis - Van Valkenberg
3. Network Lines and Fields - Ryder (Text)
4. Principle of Network Synthesis - Van Vakenberg
5. An Introduction to Digital and Analog Communication - Simon Haykin (Text)
6. Principles of Communication Systems - Taub, H and Schilling, D T
7. Modern Digital and Analog Communication Systems – Cult
