Module I - Photometry and radiometry: photometric and radiometric quantities, colour temperature, standardization of sources, theory of vision, visual basis of colorimetry
Light sources: filament lamps, halogen and fluorescent lamps, low pressure sodium and high pressure discharge lamps, all-solid-state lamps, high brightness LED, light extraction from LED, White solid-state lamps, applications of solid-sate lamps

Module II - Polarization of light: states of polarization, Jones representation, Stoke’s parameters and Poincare representation, determination of Jone’s matrices, Mueller matrices, optical anisotropy, index of ellipsoid
Optical workshop: lens grinding and polishing, optical flats, quality check of lenses and flats

Module III - Stress induced optical anisotropy: EO effect: Pockel’s and Kerr effect, Photoelastic effect and applications, Magneto optic and acousto optic effects, Raman-Nath and Bragg scattering, Faraday effects, application to modulation, e-o and m-o materials

Module IV - Polarizers and separators of polarization states: depolarizers, phase retarders, birefringent filters, optical isolators, quarter wave plates, Faraday rotators
Optical metrology: Moire patterns, surface contouring
Optical components: polarizers, compensators, optical profilers for surface roughness measurement
Measurement of Point spread function and optical transfer function of optical system
Remote sensing and its applications

Module V - Holography: inline and off axis holography, volume and phase holograms, Optical systems and light sources to record holograms, recording materials, holographic optical elements, interferometry with computer generated holograms, holographic interferometry, real time and double exposure interferometry and their applications, NDT
Speckle interferometry: electronic speckle pattern interferometry and its applications


Reference:
