Module I - Man-machine interface: need of displays, types of displays, mechanical, electrical, and electronic displays, meters and gauges, alphanumeric displays, Nixie tubes, LED displays, drive circuits, video displays

Module II - Cathode ray oscilloscopes: Cathedoluminescence, characteristics, phosphors, persistence properties, CRT, electric and magnetic deflections, deflection yokes, organization of a modern CRO, triggering and synchronization, storage oscilloscopes, digital storage oscilloscopes, TV picture tubes, colour displays.

Module III - Electroluminescence: EL phenomena, high field EL, EL phosphors, preparation of EL cells, thin film electroluminescence, TFEL displays, refresh rates, EL colour displays, active matrix displays, merits and demerits of EL displays

Module IV - Liquid crystals: physical properties of LC, molecular ordering, types of ordering in liquid crystals, nematic, cholesteric and smectic LC’s, twisted nematic phase, pitch of the ordering, homogenous and homeotropic ordering, optical properties of LC’s, light scattering and transmission properties, LC display devices, large screen LCD’s, back lighting, merits and demerits of LCD’s

Module V - Plasma Panel Displays: characteristics of plasma discharge, light emission from plasma tubes, construction of plasma element, AC plasma displays, drive voltage waveform, plasma panel drives, advantages of plasma panel displays


References:

2. Electronic Displays - S.Sherr
3. Liquid Crystals - S.Chandrasekhar
