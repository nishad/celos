Module I - General structure of atomic spectra, spectral series, Ritz combination principle, Rydberg formula, combination series, Bohr’s correspondence principle, Bohr and Bohr-Sommerfeld model, vector atom model and space quantization, quantum numbers, atomic orbitals and their shapes, expression for spin-orbit interaction, fine structure of doublet states, equivalent and nonequivalent electrons, LS and jj couplings, Hund’s rule of multiplicity, Lande’s interval rule, normal and inverted terms, atomic states, symmetry of atomic states, selection rules

Module II - Spectra of one electron systems like hydrogen and alkali atoms and two electron systems, fine structure of spectra of alkali and alkali earth atoms, intensity of spectral lines, fine structure of hydrogen spectrum, lamb shift, hyperfine spectra, anomalous and normal Zeeman effect, Lande’s g factor, Zeeman effect in the spectrum of Na atom, Intensity distribution of Zeeman components, Paschen Back effect
Microwave spectra of molecules: rotational energy levels of rigid and non rigid diatomic molecule, pure rotational structure of spectra of diatomic molecules, nature of microwave spectrum and evaluation of molecular constants

Module III - Infrared spectra of diatomic molecules, energy level structure of anharmonic oscillator, rotational structure of IR band, selection rule
Electronic spectra of diatomic molecules, coarse structure, evaluation of vibrational constants, sequences and progressions, Deslander’s table, isotope effect, Franck-Condon principle, intensity distribution of bands in absorption and emission spectra, dissociation and pre dissociation, evaluation of dissociation energy, vibrational temperature

Module IV - Rotational structure of the electronic spectra of diatomic molecules, rotational structure of the spectra, P, Q, R branches, band shading, band head formation, evaluation of rotational constants, representation and nomenclature of energy levels of diatomic molecules
Raman effect, classical and quantum descriptions, Stokes and antiStokes radiation, experimental set up, applications of Raman effect, stimulated Raman scattering,

Module V - Fluorescence and phosphorescence, thermoluminescence, Thermo optic spectroscopy, thermal lens, photoacoustic and photothermal deflection techniques (introductory ideas only), multi photon spectroscopy (introductory ideas)
Spectra of polyatomic molecules: modes of vibrations of linear triatomic and nonlinear triatomic molecules with special reference to CO2 and H2O, complementary nature of IR and Raman spectra
Breadth of spectral lines, Doppler free spectroscopy (introductory ideas only)

Advanced Reading Section - X-ray spectra, Mosley’s law, practical applications of singlet oxygen
