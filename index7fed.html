Home » Academics » Course Structure

Reading Section - Maxwell’s equations, propagation of EMW through conducting a non conducting media, wave mechanical and matrix mechanical formulation of quantum mechanics, perturbation theory, basic laser theory

Module I - Quantization of EMF, field quantization, Fock states, coherent states, coherence properties of EMF, first order optical coherence, coherent field, photon correlation measurements, photon counting measurements

Module II - Representation of EMF, expansion in number states, coherent states, P-representation, correlation and characteristic functions, photon statistics, photon number representation

Module III - Density of states and density matrix, squeezed light, generation and applications of squeezed light, coherent interaction of light with matter, Maxwell-Bloch equations, spontaneous decay of two level atoms

Module IV - Optical bistability, dispersive and absorptive cases, Bell’s inequalities in quantum optics, EPR argument, experimental studies, non demolition measurements, quantum coherence

Module V - Deflection of atoms by light, Kapitza-Dirac effect, optical Stern-Gerlach experiment, interaction between atoms and quantized fields, dressed fields, Jaynes-Cummings model


References:
1. Quantum Optics - D F Walls, G H Milburn (Text)
2. Elements of Quantum Optics - Meystre and M Sargent III (Text)
3. Optical Coherence and Quantum Optics - L Mandel and E Wolf
