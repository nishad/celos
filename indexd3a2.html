Module I - Spectroscopy technique: conventional spectroscopic recording in UV-Vis-NIR region using dispersing spectrographs, photoacoustic spectroscopy, PA effect in gases, liquids, solids, RG Theory (qualitative), design of PA spectrometer, application of PAS, evaluation of optical and thermal parameters

Module II - Thermal lens spectroscopy: focal length of thermal lens, single and double beam techniques, application of TLS, OG spectroscopy, theory and experimental techniques, applications of OGS

Module III - High resolution spectroscopy: saturation spectroscopy, Doppler free spectroscopy, single atom spectroscopy, Rydberg atoms and their spectra, applications of spectra in pollution monitoring, trace analysis, laser Raman spectroscopy, SRS, CARS, PARS

Module IV - Fluorescence spectroscopy: spectra of rare earth ions and atoms, spectra of RE ions in sulphide and fluoride crystals, phosphorescence, colour centers, fluorescence of dyes, evaluation of quantum efficiency

Module V - Plasma spectroscopy: evaluation of plasma parameters from plasma spectra, nonlinear optical spectroscopy


Reference:
1. Photoacoustic Spectroscopy –Rosencwaig (Text)
2. Thermo Optic Spectroscopy - J Sell (Text)
3. Laser Spectroscopy - Chebetyev (Text)
4. Luminescence Spectra of Rare Earth Ions - Maufraunin (Text)
5. Plasma Spectroscopy - H R Greim (Text)
