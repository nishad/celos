Nityanand Sharma is a 9th semester student of 5 Year Integrated M.Sc course in Photonics in Centre of Excellence in Lasers and Optoelectronic Sciences (CELOS). He joined CELOS in the 5th batch of the Integrated M.Sc program in July 2006 and is expected to complete the course by 2011.

Area of Interest: His area of interests includes Integrated optics, Plasmonics, Biophotonics, Nanophotonics, Semiconductor laser, Optoelectronics, and Holography.

Research Projects:

He has been the receipent of prestigious “Summer Research Fellowship” by “Indian Academy of Sciences” in the year 2010 to do his project at ‘Indian Institute of Science, Bangalore’ during May- July 2010.

  • “Synthesis and Characterization of Chalcogenide Glasses for the Fabrication of Photonic Devices” Indian Institute of Science (IISc), Bangalore, India, Duration: May - July 2010.  Supervisor: Dr. S. Asokan. In this project, glass of Ga-Ge-S material was prepared and investigated for use in the fabrication of Photonics Devices using SEM, EDAX, SRS, XRD techniques.

  • “Electromagnetic Wave Propagation through Multi-layered Dielectric Periodic Medium” Satyendra Nath Bose National Centre for Basic sciences, Kolkata, India, Duration:May - July 2009 Supervisor: Dr. M. Sanjay Kumar. In this project, band gap of one dimensional periodic stratified medium was calculated for TE and TM modes of propagation for different angular position of launching of light into system. Then it was used to calculate the reflection coefficient for Bragg's Reflector and Band Gap was observed.
  • “Optical Fiber Sensor Based on Surface Plasmon Resonance for Detection of Acrylamide” International School of Photonics, CUSAT, India. Duration:Nov-2008 to Mar-2009 Supervisor: Dr. P. Radhakrishnan, Director of CELOS, India. In this project, a fiber optics based biosensor using thermal vapor deposition technique for asymmetric coating of gold on fiber core was fabricated and characterized. We recorded the transmitted output and found that excitation of surface plasmon modes is dependent on angular position as well as the wavelength.
