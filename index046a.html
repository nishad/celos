Nanostructures for Quantum Information Processing
DTU Fysik, Technical University of Denmark, Copenhagen, Denmark (May-July 2010) Supervisor: Prof. Ulrik L. Andersen
Realization of single photon sources for Quantum Information systems. Coupling nitrogen vacancy centres in diamonds to metallic waveguides are crucial for single photon sources in optical network Primarily my work was to make metallic waveguide structures in size of micrometer or more. Gold and silver waveguides were fabricated by performing focused ion beam milling on metallic nanoflakes. The coupling to diamonds was achieved by an Atomic Force Microscope (AFM). Photon emission of NV centre coupled to metallic waveguide were taken. Primarily results obtained till now looks positive.

Investigation of electron spin of nitrogen vacancy centres in diamonds
DTU Fysik, Technical University of Denmark, Copenhagen, Denmark (May-July 2010) Supervisor: Ulrik L. Andersen 

We sought to examine the spin transition between the triplet ground and excited states. Polarization selectivity rules can be tested by subjecting to microwave frequency (~2870MHz). My role was to design and fabricate microstrip coplanar waveguides tuned to microwave frequencies. The work involved simulation of design parameters and working on photolithography masks for waveguides and vacuum deposition apparatus for gold metallic layers. Next  NV centres are placed in presence of TEM field, based on the orientation of magnetic field orientation various measurements regarding excitation and polarization are made. 

Apart  from regular academic stuff, I am involved in quite a few invigorating  research  works here at our labs.

Design, Implementation and commercialization of spectral surface plasmon resonance sensors for biomedical application. (Current)
The idea is to make a portable and accurate sensor employing the principle of SPR(Surface Plasmon Resonance) . Presence of an analyte molecule will cause a shift SPR resonance of metallic nanoparticles. A fiber based SPR sensor is made by stripping the cladding and depositing PVA and nanoparticles coating. Next we palce place in micrometer width channel. Such a sensor enable to consume micrometer amount of analyte. Trials have been performed on dummy samples and next trials would be on biological samples. 

Laser Ablation of Nobel metals nanoparticles and NLO characterization, (July 2009- January 2010)
So far we have been successful in creating in silver and gold nanoparticles in organic aqua mediums using laser ablation (Nd:YAG at 1064nm & 532nm). Also using Z scan we have found non linear absorption coefficienct and non linear refractive index. Next we seek to implement these samples and for plasmonics sensors

 

Diffusivity measurements by using photothermal method.
Using a dual beam methods we have found thermal diffusivity of Nano-composites (silver nanoparticles in Rhodaminne6G  and PPV dyes ). Photo-thermal being non destructive method is found to be apt for low  diffusivuty  measurements. Also fluorescence related related aspect has been studied.
 
Realization of optical logic gates and optical bistable functions in saturable absorbers under pulsed and continuous wave excitations
This project includes  my VIth semester mini project. We have successfully formed the NOT and NAND gate by thermal lens technique. We have covered the various aspects of our experimental setup and results. Experiments were carried out for different samples like PPV and nanocomposite of PPV and Silver. Optical bistability and its occurrence has been demonstrated through experiments using pulsed and CW lasers. Under various values of the peak on axis intensity at focus, we were able to observe different optical functions like optical switching, optical limiting and optical memory under pulsed excitation. Preliminary results in CW regime also indicated optical switching. We had attributed these to optical bistability induced by nonlinear absorptionand nonlinear refraction.
